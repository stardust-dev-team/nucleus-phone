## Demo Script (20 min)

### 1. The Pitch (2 min)

> Blake, appreciate you taking time on your day off. I'll keep this tight.
>
> We sell industrial air compressors — rotary screws, dryers, filters — to machine shops, manufacturers, body shops. B2B, exactly your wheelhouse.
>
> What I built is a phone dialer that does something nobody else has — it uses AI to research every contact before you call them, tells you exactly what to say, and handles all the CRM logging automatically. You just talk.
>
> The opportunity: commission-only, work your own hours. Evenings, days off, whenever. Every qualified lead you generate that turns into a sale, you get paid. No quota, no schedule, no pressure.

### 2. The Contact List (2 min)

**Show: Contacts page**

- Search for a company or name — show the HubSpot integration
- Point out signal tiers (Spear / Targeted / Awareness) — "The system already scored these. Red = highest priority, they're showing buying signals"
- Show timezone filtering — "You'd filter to your timezone so you're calling people during business hours, or filter to Pacific if you're calling evenings"
- Show the "Never called" vs "3d ago" badges — "You can see who needs attention"

### 3. The Call Cockpit — The Money Feature (5 min)

**Click into a contact to open the Cockpit**

Walk through each section top to bottom:

- **Contact Identity** — Name, title, company, fit score. "The system already knows who this person is, what they do, how good a fit they are"
- **Suggested Opener** (amber banner) — "This is AI-generated based on everything we know about them. It's not a script — it's a conversation starter that sounds natural"
- **Rapport Tags** — "Quick-hit talking points. Their background, shared interests, things to mention"
- **Intel Nuggets** — "This is the deep stuff. What equipment they probably run, what problems they likely have, what emails they've opened. You'd need 20 minutes of research to find this — you get it in 2 seconds"
- **Watch Outs** (orange) — "Things NOT to say. Just as important as what to say"
- **Adapted Script** — "Not a word-for-word script. More like a game plan — lead with this angle, avoid that topic, here's why"
- **Company Intel** — Revenue, employee count, industry signals
- **Product Reference** — "Which of our products fit this customer. You don't need to memorize the catalog — it's right here"
- **Email Engagement** — "Can see which emails they opened, what they clicked. So you know what they're already interested in before you dial"

### 4. Making a Call (2 min)

**Show but don't actually call (it's Sunday)**

- Point to the Call button — "One click. It dials through our (602) number, records everything automatically"
- Show the Mute / Keypad controls
- Mention: "Calls are recorded and transcribed by AI. After every call you just tag the outcome — connected, voicemail, callback — add a quick note, done. It syncs to HubSpot and Slack automatically"

### 5. The Scoreboard (2 min)

**Navigate to Scoreboard**

- Show the leaderboard with sparklines — "This is how the team tracks activity. Calls made, leads qualified, hot leads, average call length"
- "Friendly competition. You can see who's putting in work and who's generating results"

### 6. The Pipeline (2 min)

**Navigate to Pipeline**

- Show signal tiers with revenue estimates — "These are companies our system identified as likely buyers. Spear tier = highest confidence"
- "You're not cold calling from a phonebook. Every contact has been scored and researched before it ever hits your screen"

### 7. The Deal (3 min)

> Here's how the money works. Commission-only — you call when you want, as much or as little as you want. Every qualified lead that converts to a sale, you earn a commission on that deal.
>
> What makes this different from any phone gig you've had: the AI does the homework. You don't need to know anything about air compressors on day one. The cockpit tells you what to say, what to ask, what to avoid. Your job is to be a human being on the phone — build rapport, qualify the need, and hand it off.
>
> With your B2B background — especially the SaaS and defense contracting stuff Britt mentioned — you already know how to have a consultative conversation. That's the hard part. The product knowledge is built into the tool.

## If He Asks...

**"How much per sale?"**
> Our compressor packages range from $8K to $60K+. Commission structure depends on deal size — we can nail down specifics once you see the pipeline and get a feel for the product. It's meaningful money on every closed deal.

**"How many calls to get a sale?"**
> Varies, but the signal scoring means you're not dialing blind. A good caller working 5-10 hours a week can generate 2-3 qualified leads. Conversion from qualified to closed is where Alex (our sales lead) takes over — you hand off the warm lead, he closes it.

**"Do I need to know about compressors?"**
> No. The cockpit gives you everything. Product reference, sizing, even what to recommend. Your job is the human connection — the AI handles the technical prep.

**"What hours?"**
> Whenever you want. The system shows timezone-adjusted calling windows. Evenings work great for reaching shop owners who are busy during the day. Your Sunday/random weekday off is perfect for daytime calls.
