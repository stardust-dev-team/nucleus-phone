## Likely Questions & Answers

### "How much per sale, realistically?"

"Depends on the deal. On a small 10HP system you're looking at five to six hundred bucks per deal. On a 100HP permanent magnet VFD deal you're looking at thirty-seven hundred plus. System bundles push the PM VFD numbers to five thousand or more. The honest answer is the volume of small deals plus one big deal per quarter gets you into real money."

### "How many calls to get a sale?"

"The signal scoring means you're not dialing blind, so conversion is way better than traditional cold calling. A good caller working five to ten hours a week tends to generate two to three qualified leads. Close rate on qualified leads is thirty to forty percent. Do the math: that's roughly one to two deals a week working part time."

### "Do I need to know about compressors?"

"Zero. Day one, you know nothing about compressors. The cockpit tells you what to say, what to recommend, what to avoid. By month two you'll know enough to have most conversations without referring to it. The technical backup comes from CAS engineers and Alex when you need it."

### "What hours work best?"

"Shop owners are easier to reach before 8 AM or after 5 PM because they're on the floor during the day. Evenings are gold. Your Sunday is actually a great day for Pacific time zone shops because they're open Sunday afternoons. Your random weekday off is perfect for daytime calls to the rest of the country."

### "How do I get paid?"

"Commission is paid after the deal closes and the customer pays us. Standard industrial B2B terms are net 30 or net 60, so you'd typically see your commission 45 to 75 days after the verbal close. We can work out exact payment schedule when we sign you up."

### "Is there any exclusivity or territory?"

"No territory. You work the whole country. The only thing we watch for is duplicate outreach — we don't want you and Alex both calling the same contact, so the system assigns contacts to prevent that. First-come-first-served on the signal pipeline."

### "What if the lead I hand to Alex closes?"

"You get a smaller finder fee on handed-off leads. Not the full ten percent, because you didn't close it, but you still get paid for generating the qualified lead. Exact split we can work out."

### "Can I bring my own leads?"

"Absolutely. If you've got connections in the industry from your past sales work, bring them in. Any deal you close, you get the full commission regardless of where the lead came from. Just don't call anyone you're servicing at your current dealership job — that's the one off-limits rule, for both our protection."

### "What's the catch?"

"The only catch is it's commission only. No base salary. If you don't put in the work, you don't get paid. But you've done commission sales before and you know that tradeoff. The upside is no cap, your own hours, and a tool that does ninety percent of the hard work for you."
